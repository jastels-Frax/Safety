Incident & Near-Miss Reporting

Describes how to report workplace incidents, injuries, and near-miss events in a timely manner.

Reporting Timeline

  • Minor injuries and near misses — report to direct supervisor within 24 hours
  • Lost-time injuries — notify the Safety Officer within 4 hours
  • Critical injuries or fatalities — call emergency services immediately, then notify management

How to Report

Complete the Incident Report Form in the Submissions tab. A supervisor review must be completed within 48 hours of submission.

Company to insert: email address and any additional reporting channels (e.g., online portal, paper form location).

Corrective Actions

The project manager is responsible for investigating root causes and implementing corrective controls within 10 business days. All corrective actions are tracked in the safety log.

PPE Requirements

Specifies mandatory personal protective equipment for each project type and site condition.

Minimum Requirements — All Sites

  • Hard hat — CSA Z94.1 Class E
  • Safety-toed footwear — CSA Z195
  • High-visibility vest or outerwear — Class 2 minimum
  • UV-protective safety glasses where applicable

Project-Specific Requirements

Company to insert: site-specific PPE matrix by project type (e.g., chainsaw chaps, bear spray, life jackets, half-face respirators, fall-arrest harnesses).

Inspection & Replacement

All PPE must be visually inspected before each use. Damaged or expired equipment must be removed from service immediately, tagged "Out of Service," and reported to the project supervisor.

Lone Worker & Remote Work Policy

Safety requirements and check-in procedures for personnel working alone or in remote field locations.

Applicability

This policy applies to any Fraxinus employee working where they cannot be directly observed by a co-worker, or where access to emergency services may be delayed by more than 20 minutes.

Check-In Requirements

  • Check in with a designated contact at the start and end of each field day
  • Missed check-ins trigger escalation after a defined interval
  • Approved communication devices must be carried at all times in remote areas

Standard procedures for collecting, recording, and backing up environmental and geomatics field data.

Data Quality Standards

All field-collected data must be legible, dated, and attributed to a named collector. Corrections must be made with a single strike-through — never use white-out on paper field sheets.

  • Paper field sheets — scanned and uploaded within 48 hours of collection

Equipment Pre-Use Inspection

Describes pre-use inspection requirements and scheduled maintenance intervals for all field equipment.

Daily Inspection Requirements

All powered equipment (ATVs, generators, power tools, survey instruments) must be visually inspected before first use each day and documented on the Field Equipment Log.

  • Check fluid levels, belts, and safety guards
  • Test all controls and emergency stop functions
  • Confirm equipment registration and insurance are current

Defect Reporting

Any defect that could create a safety risk must be reported to the supervisor immediately. Equipment must be tagged "Out of Service" and removed from use until repaired and cleared by a qualified person.

Scheduled Maintenance

Company to insert: maintenance schedule by equipment type (engine hours or calendar intervals), service provider contacts, and maintenance log location.
